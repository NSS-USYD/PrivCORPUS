What is personal data?
Personal data refers to any information that pertains to an identifiable or identified individual. This means that an individual can be identified, either directly or indirectly, by such information, which could include an identification number or any factors specific to their physical, physiological, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity.
Special categories of data, also known as sensitive data, include information that reveals an individual's racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade-union membership, and data concerning health or sex life.
