Definitions
Dolice is owned by Masaki Hirokawa, based in Tokyo - Japan. (referred to as "I", "Me", "Our" or "We"). As a customer of this service you're a "User" or "You" according to this policy. The applications or any services offered by us will be referred to as "Dolice", "App", "Website" or "Service".
How does Dolice collect data about me?
Dolice collects data about you:
when you browse the Dolice website,
dolice.net
when you use the Dolice apps (This data will only be used for the purpose of improving the stability and convenience of the application)
when you send support, privacy, legal, and other requests to us
We don't buy or receive data about you from data brokers or other private services.
What data does Dolice collect about me, and why?
Dolice collects data about how you use Dolice service.
Dolice logs data that might be identified to you:
a random, unique identifier
the version of the Dolice app, and the operating system you are using
Application crash log
Date and time, data such as country and region
Dolice uses this data to:
keep our service working quickly and reliably
debug and develop our apps and website
defend Dolice service from abuse and technical attacks
improve usability of our apps
Dolice collects data about how you use the website.
When you visit
dolice.net
and other Dolice websites, Dolice uses server logs, and other methods to collect data about what pages you visit, and when. Dolice also collects technical information about the software and computer you use, such as:
your IP address
your preferred language
the web browser software you use
the kind of computer you use
the website that referred you
Dolice uses data about how you use the website to:
optimize the website, so that it's quick and easy to use
diagnose and debug technical errors
defend the website from abuse and technical attacks
compile statistics on the kinds of software and computers visitors use
Dolice collects data about correspondence.
Dolice collects data about you when you send Dolice support requests, legal complaints, privacy inquiries, and business inquiries. Those data usually include your name and email address, and may include your company or other affiliation.
Dolice uses contact data to:
respond to you
improve the service
defend Dolice from legal claims
Dolice stores correspondence as long as it may be useful for these purposes.
Does Dolice comply with the EU General Data Protection Regulation?
Dolice respects privacy rights under
Regulation (EU) 2016/679
, the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
Information that GDPR requires Dolice to give can be found throughout these privacy questions and answers.
So can also possible to send inquiries and requests about specific rights, like access, rectification, erasure, data portability, and
objection to automated decision-making
.
GDPR does not apply to everyone worldwide.
But Dolice's policy is to do its best to offer all users the same privacy information, control, and protections, whether GDPR applies to them or not.
Does Dolice share data about me with others?
Dolice does not sell information about you to others. However, Dolice uses services provided by other companies and is working to improve websites and applications.
Some of these services may be used for the purpose of gathering information necessary for improving the convenience of websites and applications.

Does Dolice make automated decisions based on data about me?
No. Dolice does not use your data for personalization or retargeting purposes.
